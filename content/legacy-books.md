## A life, told in their own voice

A legacy book is not a generic biography assembled from dates. It is a person's stories, humor, values, and way of speaking, shaped into a book that still sounds like them.

We begin with relaxed recorded conversations. Then we organize the memories, write the chapters, select and caption photographs, design the pages, and guide the family through revisions. You do not need to arrive with an outline or a box of perfectly organized photos.

## Choose the right size

A short **Legacy Letter** can preserve a few defining stories and messages. A **Mini Legacy Book** can cover the major chapters of a life. A **Full Legacy Book** gives a whole life room to breathe, including photographs, documents, a timeline, and family history.

## Good reasons to begin

Families often start for a milestone birthday, an anniversary, a retirement, a health change, or simply because they have realized how many questions remain unasked. The best time is while the storyteller can still choose the stories and tell them in their own words.

## What the process feels like

The interviews are conversations, not interrogations. We follow the memories that light someone up, pause when they need a break, and invite family members to fill in details without taking over the storyteller's voice.

When the draft is ready, your family reviews it. We correct facts, refine the wording, and make sure the final book feels true before it is printed.
