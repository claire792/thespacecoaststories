Memory, Loss, and Legacy is a collection of gentle, practical resources for families facing illness, death, grief, or the work of remembering. The articles cover recording stories when time is short, writing eulogies that sound human, creating tribute books, planning celebrations of life, preserving letters and journals, and deciding what to keep after someone dies.

There is no perfect way to document a person, especially during a difficult season. A few clear memories, a saved voice, a familiar phrase, or the story behind one photograph can become deeply important later. This series helps families focus on those specific details instead of feeling pressure to capture an entire life at once.

Start with the article that matches what you are facing today. The rest can wait.
