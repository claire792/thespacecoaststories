A family-story project becomes much easier when the first questions are practical. Ask a Historian brings together straightforward answers from a working personal historian: how to interview a parent or grandparent, organize photographs, compare memoir services, estimate costs, use AI carefully, and decide what kind of finished keepsake fits your family.

These guides are written for people who care about preserving a life but do not necessarily think of themselves as writers or historians. You can begin with a phone recording, a notebook, a stack of photographs, or one conversation at the kitchen table. The important part is creating a process that feels comfortable enough for the storyteller to keep talking.

Use this series as a starting point, then follow the links inside each article for deeper help with the next step.
