To choose a personal historian, look at their sample work, ask how they conduct interviews and preserve a person's voice, understand exactly what's included and what it costs, and make sure you feel comfortable with them, because they'll be sitting with someone you love. Below are the ten questions to ask before you hire one, plus the red flags to watch for.

Hiring someone to tell your mother's life story is a tender decision. This person will spend hours with a loved one, hear private memories, and shape them into something your family keeps forever. You want to choose well.

The good news: a few good questions tell you almost everything. Here's how to find the right person, and how to spot the wrong one.

## First, what a personal historian actually does

If you're new to the term, a personal historian is a professional who preserves people's life stories, usually by interviewing them and turning those conversations into a book, audio, or video. We wrote a fuller explainer in [what is a personal historian](/blog/what-is-a-personal-historian). This post is about choosing a good one.

## The 10 questions to ask before you hire

Ask these on a first call. How someone answers tells you as much as what they answer.

1. **Can I see samples of your work?** The single most important question. Real samples show you their writing, their design, and whether the finished books feel alive. Be cautious if they can't show any.

2. **How do you conduct interviews?** Listen for warmth and patience. You want someone who draws people out gently, not a checklist reader. Ask how many sessions they typically do.

3. **How do you preserve the person's own voice?** A good historian keeps your loved one sounding like themselves, not like a generic biography. Ask how they protect those real phrases and rhythms.

4. **What exactly is included?** Pin down the scope: interview hours, transcription, writing, editing, photo work, design, and printing. Know what's in the price and what costs extra.

5. **What does it cost, and how is it structured?** Get it clear and in writing. Ask about deposits, payment stages, and what happens if the project grows. See our [cost breakdown](/blog/cost-to-hire-memoir-writer) for context on ranges.

6. **How long does it take?** Understand the timeline, especially if a loved one's health makes time tight. Ask what they can do quickly if needed.

7. **Who owns the material and the final files?** Make sure your family keeps the rights and the recordings, and ask how they handle your privacy.

8. **Can you work with fading memory or health issues?** If relevant, ask about their experience with hearing loss, low energy, or dementia. It takes a special skill and patience.

9. **How many copies do we get, and can we order more later?** Clarify what's included and how reprints work, so the book can reach the whole family.

10. **What happens if we're not happy?** A confident professional will have a thoughtful answer about revisions and making it right.

## What to look for in a good personal historian

Beyond the answers, notice how they make you feel. The right person is warm, curious, and genuinely interested in your family, an easy person to imagine sitting across from your mother.

Look for real samples you love, clear and honest pricing, patience, good listening, and respect for the person's own voice. Experience helps, but warmth and care matter just as much. Your loved one has to trust them, or the stories won't come.

## Red flags to watch for

- No samples, or vague ones
- Pricing that's murky or keeps shifting
- Rushing you, or rushing the interviews
- More interested in a slick product than in your loved one
- Can't explain how they keep the person's voice
- No contract or unclear ownership of your family's material

## Is hiring a personal historian worth it?

For most families, especially when time is short, yes. You end up with a permanent record of a loved one's voice and life, done with a care and skill that's hard to manage yourself while also being their child or grandchild. It's one of the few purchases that grows more precious with every passing year.

That said, the right answer is the honest one: if you have the time, the skill, and a loved one who opens up easily to you, doing it yourself is a beautiful path too. Choose based on your family, not on anyone's sales pitch.

## Frequently asked questions

**What should I look for when hiring a personal historian?**
Look for real sample work you love, a warm and patient interviewing style, clear and honest pricing, respect for the person's own voice, and someone your loved one will feel comfortable with. Ask to see finished books and to understand exactly what's included.

**What questions should I ask before hiring someone to write a life story?**
Ask to see samples, how they interview, how they preserve the person's voice, what's included, the full cost and timeline, who owns the material, whether they can work with health or memory issues, how many copies you get, and what happens if you're not satisfied.

**How do I find someone to write my mother's life story?**
Search for a personal historian or memoir writer, ideally one near you or experienced with remote interviews, and vet them with the ten questions in this post. Prioritize warmth, real samples, and clear pricing. A comfortable rapport matters, because they'll be spending hours with your mother.

**Is hiring a personal historian worth the money?**
For most families it is, particularly when time is limited. You get a lasting record of a loved one's voice and life, made with professional skill. If you have the time and your loved one opens up easily to you, doing it yourself is also a valid, meaningful option.
