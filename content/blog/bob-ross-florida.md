*Published July 2, 2026 · Updated July 2, 2026*

Bob Ross, the gentle painter behind The Joy of Painting and its "happy little trees," was born in Daytona Beach, Florida, in 1942 and raised in Orlando. Before the soothing voice and the PBS fame, he spent twenty years in the Air Force. His calm on-camera persona was a deliberate choice. Here's the Florida story behind the man.

That soft voice. The happy little trees. The most soothing half hour on television. Millions know Bob Ross. Far fewer know he was a Florida boy, or that the gentle man on screen was, in part, a decision he made about who he wanted to be.

## The short version

- **October 29, 1942** — Born in Daytona Beach, Florida
- **Childhood** — Raised in Orlando; his father was a carpenter and his mother a waitress
- **1961–1981** — Serves twenty years in the U.S. Air Force, much of it in Alaska
- **Alaska years** — Discovers painting and masters the fast wet-on-wet technique
- **1983–1994** — Hosts The Joy of Painting on PBS
- **July 4, 1995** — Dies in Orlando at age 52

## Three turning points

**A Florida boyhood.** Ross was born in Daytona Beach and grew up in Orlando in a working family, his father a carpenter, his mother a waitress. Florida made him before Alaska and PBS ever did.

**Finding painting in the Air Force.** Ross spent two decades in the military, rising to master sergeant, much of it stationed in Alaska. It was there, far from Florida, that he took an art class and discovered the wet-on-wet technique that let him finish a whole landscape in under thirty minutes. The snowy Alaskan wilderness became the mountains and trees he'd paint for the rest of his life.

**Deciding to be gentle.** Here's the detail most people don't know. Ross's military roles had required him to be tough and stern. When he left the service, he reportedly decided he was done raising his voice, that he'd never yell again. The calm, kind persona the world adored wasn't just his nature. It was a choice, made by a man who'd spent twenty years being the opposite.

## How Florida shaped him

Ross's roots were pure Florida: Daytona Beach, Orlando, a working-class family. And Florida is where he returned and where his story ended. The wilderness he painted was Alaskan, but the man holding the brush was shaped by a Florida childhood and family.

## What his story teaches us about our own

Here's what gets me about Bob Ross, in the work I do.

The public knew one Bob Ross: gentle, soft-spoken, endlessly kind. But there was a whole other life underneath, a Florida boyhood, twenty years in uniform, a stern master sergeant who made a deliberate decision to become the calm man we remember. The soothing voice had a backstory, and the backstory is the richer tale.

Everyone you love has that same depth. The grandmother everyone thinks of as sweet and quiet lived a whole life before you knew her, made choices, survived things, decided who to become. The public version of a person, even within a family, is only the surface. The real story, how they became the person you know, is what's worth capturing. Because once they're gone, only the surface remains unless someone recorded the rest.
