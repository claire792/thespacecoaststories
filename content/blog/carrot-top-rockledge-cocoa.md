*Published July 7, 2026 · Updated July 7, 2026*

Carrot Top, the red-haired prop comedian, was born in Rockledge, Florida, in 1965 and grew up in nearby Cocoa as the son of a NASA engineer. The Space Coast is where Scott Thompson, his real name, spent his childhood before building one of the most unusual and durable careers in comedy. Here's his Brevard County beginning.

You know him by the wild red hair and the trunk full of crazy props. You probably don't know his name is Scott Thompson, or that he's a Space Coast kid whose dad helped build rockets.

Carrot Top is Brevard County through and through. Here's the story.

## The short version

- **February 25, 1965** — Scott Christopher Thompson is born in Rockledge, Florida
- **Childhood** — Grows up in Cocoa, Florida; his father is a NASA engineer
- **1983** — Graduates from Cocoa High School
- **Late 1980s** — Performs his first stand-up as a college freshman at Florida Atlantic University
- **1991** — Gets his big break on Comic Strip Live
- **2005** — Begins his long-running Las Vegas headlining residency at the Luxor

## Three turning points

**Growing up the son of a NASA engineer.** Carrot Top's father worked as an engineer in the space program, which makes him a true child of the Space Coast, raised in Cocoa during the era of launches and moon shots. It's a striking contrast: the precise, technical world of his rocket-engineer father, and the loud, chaotic, prop-filled comedy the son would go on to invent.

**His first stand-up set in college.** As a freshman at Florida Atlantic University, Thompson tried stand-up for the first time. That first set lit the fuse. He'd found the thing he was meant to do, and he chased it hard, even working as a courier to get by in the early days.

**The Luxor residency.** In 2005, Carrot Top began headlining in Las Vegas at the Luxor, and he never really stopped. That residency turned a novelty prop act into one of the most durable careers on the Strip, running for years and years. The kid from Cocoa became a Vegas institution.

## How the Space Coast shaped him

Carrot Top grew up in Cocoa in the thick of the space age, in a household built around his father's engineering work. That's a very Space Coast childhood, one shaped by the industry that defined the whole region.

And there's something fitting in it. A prop comic is, in his own strange way, an inventor and a tinkerer, building contraptions and gadgets for a living. The engineer's son didn't follow his father into the firing room, but he inherited the tinkerer's instinct and pointed it somewhere no one expected.

## What his story teaches us about our own

Here's what strikes me, doing the work I do.

Carrot Top's story only makes sense when you know where he came from: a Space Coast home, an engineer father, a specific Cocoa childhood. The famous version, the props and the hair, is the surface. The real story is the family and the place that made him.

That's true for everyone. Your quirks, your work, your whole personality traces back to a particular family in a particular place, and those roots are the most interesting part of anyone's story. But they're also the first thing forgotten once the "official" version takes over. The story of who raised you, and where, and what they did for a living, is worth capturing while the people who lived it can still tell it.
