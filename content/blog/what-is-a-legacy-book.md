A legacy book is a printed book built from someone's life story, told in their own words.

That's the short version. The longer version is that it's a series of relaxed interviews, shaped into readable chapters, wrapped around the family photos, and printed into something your kids and their kids can pull off a shelf fifty years from now. It's not a scrapbook and it's not a memoir written alone at a desk. It's the real, spoken story of a real person, caught before it fades.

## Why people make them

Almost everyone who calls us says a version of the same thing. *I keep meaning to write down Dad's stories, and I never do.* Or the harder one: *I waited too long with my mother, and I don't want to make that mistake twice.*

That's the honest reason legacy books exist. Good intentions don't survive busy lives. A project with a start date, a process, and someone else doing the hard part does.

Here in Brevard, we see a few situations again and again. A milestone birthday coming up and nobody knows what to give. A parent moving out of the family home. A grandparent who's suddenly telling the same great story at every dinner, and a grandchild old enough to realize they should be writing it down. Any of those is a good reason. So is no reason at all except that the person is still here to ask.

## What actually goes into one

The work is simpler than it sounds, mostly because you're not the one doing it.

We start with a conversation to figure out who the book is for and what matters most. Then we interview, in person or by phone, recorded so nobody's scrambling to take notes. We ask the questions that open people up, and we follow the good tangents. After that, we do the writing, the editing, and the design. You review a draft, we revise, and then it goes to print.

Your parent talks. We build the book. That's the whole shape of it.

## What it costs

It depends on how big a life you want to capture, and we keep the pricing plain.

A short **Legacy Letter** runs a few hundred dollars and comes out of one or two conversations. A **Mini Legacy Book**, which is what most families choose, is a real book of forty to eighty pages and runs between fifteen hundred and twenty-five hundred dollars. A **Full Legacy Book**, the complete hardcover life, starts around five thousand. Book projects can be paid in stages, so nobody has to hand over the whole amount up front.

## The part nobody regrets

Here's the thing we've noticed. Families sometimes hesitate over the cost or the timing. Nobody, not once, has finished a book and wished they hadn't done it.

What they say instead is that they heard stories they'd never heard. That a quiet parent turned out to have a whole inner life nobody had asked about. That the grandkids now have their great-grandmother's voice on the page, in her own words, saved.
