A legacy book is a printed keepsake that captures a person's life story in their own words, usually built from recorded interviews, photographs, and family history. It's more personal than a formal biography and deeper than a photo album. Put simply, it's a way to keep someone's voice, and the shape of their whole life, after they're gone.

The name gets used a few different ways, so let me be clear about what I mean by it. A legacy book isn't a scrapbook and it isn't a stiff, official biography. It's the story of a real person, told the way they'd tell it, printed in a form your family can hold and hand down.

## What's actually in a legacy book

Every one is different, because every life is. But most include a few common things.

1. **Their story, in their voice.** Drawn from interviews, so the phrasing sounds like them, not like a Wikipedia entry.
2. **Photographs.** Old ones, restored where they need it, placed alongside the parts of the story they belong to.
3. **The turning points.** Childhood, love, work, the hard years, the moments that made them who they are.
4. **The small, ordinary details.** A favorite meal, a bad first car, a saying they always used. This is the stuff that brings a person back to life on the page.
5. **A real, printed book.** Softcover or hardcover, one copy or forty, made to survive being read and reread.

## Who a legacy book is for

Anyone with a story worth keeping, which is everyone. But a few situations bring people to my door more than others.

Families with an aging parent or grandparent, who feel the clock ticking. Adult children who realize they don't actually know how their parents met, or what their grandmother's childhood was like. People marking a big birthday or anniversary who want a gift that isn't another thing to dust. And families who've just lost someone, and want to gather what's left of the story before it scatters.

The quiet ones, especially. The relative who never talks about themselves often has the richest story in the room. They just need someone to ask.

## How a legacy book is different from a memoir, a biography, or a photo album

These words get tangled together, so here's the plain version.

A **memoir** is usually written by the person themselves, about a particular slice of their life. A legacy book is built *with* someone, often by a personal historian doing the writing, and it tends to cover the whole life rather than one theme.

A **biography** is typically written about someone, often after the fact, in a formal outside voice. A legacy book keeps the person's own voice at the center. It reads like them.

A **photo album** shows the pictures but not the stories behind them. A legacy book puts the story and the photo together, so the image of your grandfather at nineteen comes with the tale of what he was doing that summer.

If you want a shorthand: a legacy book is the story, the voice, and the pictures, made permanent, in one place.

## How one gets made

The path is simpler than most people expect. It starts with conversations, usually recorded over a few sessions. Those get shaped into a written story, checked with the family for names and dates, laid out with the photographs, and printed. You end up with a finished book, and often a set of copies for the whole family.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is a legacy book?**
A legacy book is a printed keepsake that tells a person's life story in their own words, built from recorded interviews, photographs, and family history. It preserves both the story and the voice of the person for future generations.

**What's the difference between a legacy book and a memoir?**
A memoir is usually written by the person themselves and focuses on part of their life. A legacy book is typically created with the help of a personal historian, covers the whole life, and keeps the subject's own voice at the center.

**How is a legacy book different from a photo album?**
A photo album shows the pictures; a legacy book tells the stories behind them. It weaves the photographs together with the person's own account of their life, so the images come with the memories that give them meaning.

**How do you make a legacy book?**
It's built from recorded interviews, which are shaped into a written story, checked with the family, laid out with photographs, and printed as a finished book. A personal historian can handle the whole process, or you can capture the interviews yourself and hire help for the writing and design.
