## Service is more than a list of assignments

The story may begin with why they enlisted or were drafted, but it should also preserve the friends, the food, the barracks, the letters home, the first day back, and the way service shaped everything that followed.

Interviews move at the veteran's pace. Pride, comedy, boredom, grief, and silence all have a place, and no one is asked to revisit something they do not want to discuss.
