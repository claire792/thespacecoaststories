You do not need a perfect plan, professional equipment, or a complete family tree to begin. You need one person, one question, and a way to save the answer. The guides below cover the first decisions families usually face: whom to interview, what to ask, how to record a conversation, how to organize old photographs, and how to keep the project moving when a relative is shy or unsure.

Start with the oldest or most time-sensitive storyteller, but let the conversation follow what matters to them. Dates and names are useful; the details that make a life recognizable are even more valuable. Ask about the house they grew up in, the work they were proud of, the choices that changed everything, and the people they still miss.

These articles are designed to help you take the next small step today, whether you are creating a simple recording, researching your family tree, or gathering material for a full legacy book.
