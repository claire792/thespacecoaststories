A legacy book turns a person's memories, photographs, letters, and voice into something a family can keep and pass down. The articles in this section explain what that process looks like from the first conversation through writing, revision, design, and printing.

You will find practical comparisons between do-it-yourself services, guided programs, AI tools, memoir writers, and personal historians. There are also answers about cost, timelines, interviewing, photo selection, and the difference between a short keepsake and a full-length life story.

There is no single correct format. Some families need a small book in time for a milestone or memorial. Others want a detailed hardcover history built over several months. The best choice depends on the storyteller, the amount of material available, and how much help the family wants. Use these guides to decide what fits before investing time or money.
